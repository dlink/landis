Frequently asked questions

Click the Q to view its answer.

  • What metals do you buy?


    • We purchase any scrap that might contain the precious metals gold, palladium or platinum. Unfortunately, titanium and chrome cobalt have no precious metal value and we do not buy amalgam.


  • Do you buy amalgam?


    • Unfortunately, no. Its value is negligible and there are strict handling procedures. We can refer you to a company that specializes in amalgam processing. Contact us for more information.


  • Do you buy partials?


    • It depends. If the metal part of the partial looks yellow to the eye then it potentially has precious metal value. If the metal looks silver, then it does not contain any precious metals. It will always be some sort of steel derivative such as chrome cobalt or titanium.


  • Can teeth be attached to the scrap?


    • Yes. Any non-metal material is burnt off during the melt process.


  • Is there a minimum amount required when mailing scrap?


    • No (with the exception of filings and orthodontics). That's the simplicity of our mail-in program!


  • How can I get an estimate of my scrap's value? I’ve never sold before.


    • Please call 800-433-6192, fax us at 631-728-3642 or email us before sending your package to request a free estimate and enclose a note in your box as back up. We need to be informed of your request before your package arrives at our facility so that we can flag your account and handle your package accordingly. If for any reason you’d like your scrap returned we pay the postage to ship it back to you in its original form.


  • How long does it take before we receive payment?


    • Upon receipt of merchandise, payment is usually made within two weeks. Our only exceptions are filings,sweeps and orthodontic material which take a little longer to process.


  • Do I have enough scrap for a coin?


    • Most likely. We have coins available as small as one tenth of an ounce.


  • Do nongold or "white" crown and bridge have any value? Should I bother saving this material?


    • Yes, you should! Usually it is a mixture known as "semiprecious," which contains silver, palladium and a small percentage of gold.


  • Can I order more than one collecting jar? I have multiple operatories and would like one at each station.


    • The containers are free and you can order as many as you like. Order your free containers here.


  • Can a pickup be arranged at my office? I'd rather not mail the jar.


    • Of course. We'll be happy to schedule a free, insured UPS pickup on your behalf. We only need one day's advance notice, before 3 pm EST.


  • Will you buy a patient's single crown or bridge? If yes, how do we send it to you?


    • Yes, we have a program geared for small quantities. Order free Patient Scrap Mailers .


  • Do I need to fill out any type of paperwork?


    • No. However, to better serve you, clearly write your name and address under your office’s mailcode found on the upper left hand corner of the return label. Please do not cover or remove the mailcode regardless of who is mailing the package. Inside the box circle your payment option on the label. If you have any special requests please contact us by phone, email, or fax before mailing so that we can flag your account prior to receipt of your package. You may enclose a note as back up.


  • Do you purchase jewelry?


    • Yes. We purchase karat gold jewelry. We melt it down and pay on the pure content of the scrap which is usually less than the retail purchase price. If your jewelry contains stones you would like to keep, please have them removed before mailing. Please do not send costume jewelry, gold filled and gold plated jewelry as they would contain none to negligible amounts of gold, too low to process. Dentists, staff, and patients can take advantage of this service.


  • Do you buy lead?


    • No. Lead does not contain any precious metal.


  • I forgot to write my address information on the box, how will you know it is mine?


    • Every pre-paid mailer, MRS and BRM label has an identifying mailcode for each doctor at an office. If anyone mailed in without adding their contact information we can usually find them with assistance from the dental office. Please leave the handwritten four digit mailcode, which is located at the top left hand corner of the return label, visible.


  • Do you buy any electronic scrap containing precious metals?


    • Unfortunately no.
